Academics

I study math at Harvard, with a particular interest in algebra (Math 55A) and probability. I was named a John Harvard Scholar (2023 - 2024) and am currently a course assistant for Math 154 (Probability Theory) during spring of 2025.

Research

  • Cost Allocation in Nepenthes pitcher plants: project mentored by Chris Thorogood and Henry Cerbone (Department of Botany, Oxford).
  • Harvester Ants: Surveying and excavating colonies in the Chiricahua desert with the Gordon Lab (Stanford).
  • Leafcutter Ants: During high school, I grew several colonies of desert leafcutter ants Acromyrmex versicolor, testing endemic antimicrobials and observing foraging behavior.

Music

I’m currently pursuing a concurrent M.M. in classical guitar under Eliot Fisk at the New England Conservatory of Music, where I’m a Dean’s Scholarship recipient. Here’s my program bio:

Reade Park, described as “a rare and wonderful talent” by guitar luminary Sharon Isbin, is a musician from San Francisco, California. The first prize winner at the Guitar Foundation of America’s 2022 International Youth Competition, Reade received second prize in the 2024 Boston GuitarFest and 2023 University of Rhode Island International Open-Division Competitions, and is a two-time National YoungArts Foundation Award Winner. He appeared on NPR’s From The Top in 2021 and three times on the OMNI Foundation’s Young Virtuosos Series, most recently in 2024.

As a composer, Reade received Harvard University’s 2024 John Green Prize, was a Finalist in the 2022 ASCAP Morton Gould Young Composers Competition, and received 1st Prize in the San Francisco Conservatory of Music’s 2021 Kris Getz Composition Competition.

Reade also performs with the Harvard Callbacks, recently qualifying for Quarterfinals at the 2025 International Championship of Collegiate A Cappella. His debut arrangement and performance of the Arctic Monkeys’ “Baby I’m Yours” rendition garnered acclaim online (10M views across platforms).
